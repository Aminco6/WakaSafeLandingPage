Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about WakaSafe — from PINs to offline SMS

🔹 How does WakaSafe work without internet? ▼
WakaSafe uses native SMS (text messages) to send your live GPS location. Once you set up your journey and grant SMS permission, it works completely offline on any Nigerian network — MTN, Airtel, Glo, 9mobile. No data, Wi-Fi, or internet connection required.
🔹 How do I activate a journey? ▼
Steps to activate:
1. Fill in your journey details: origin, destination, vehicle type, and number plate.
2. Set your preferred SMS interval (e.g., every 15 minutes).
3. Enter your 4-digit Start PIN to begin the journey.
4. A confirmation SMS is sent to all emergency contacts.
🔹 What are the three PINs used for? ▼
🟢 Start PIN – activates the journey and sends the first location broadcast.
🔵 End PIN – safely ends the journey and sends a final “arrived safely” SMS with your location and duration.
🔴 Distress PIN – a secret PIN used under threat. It looks like a normal end journey but silently sends an SOS emergency alert to all contacts without showing anything on screen.
🔹 How do I turn on SMS settings? ▼
Go to Settings → SMS Settings inside the app. From there you can:
• Enable/disable SMS alerts
• Choose your preferred interval (10, 15, 30, or 60 minutes)
• Manage which contacts receive updates
🔹 Can my family track me in real-time? ▼
Yes! Every SMS update contains a live Google Maps link with your exact latitude/longitude. Your contacts can click the link and see your location in real-time. They do NOT need to install any app — it works on any phone with a web browser.
🔹 What happens if I lose my phone during a journey? ▼
As long as the journey is active, the phone has battery, and there is network coverage, WakaSafe will continue sending location updates at your chosen interval using the last known PIN. Your contacts will still receive updates until the battery dies or the journey times out.

🔹 How many emergency contacts can I add? ▼
You can add as many emergency contacts as you want. Every SMS update is sent to all contacts simultaneously. Add family, friends, or colleagues — anyone you trust to monitor your journey.
🔹 What information is included in the SMS? ▼
Each SMS includes:
• Journey origin and destination
• Vehicle type and number plate
• Estimated time of arrival (ETA)
• Elapsed time
• Live Google Maps link with GPS coordinates
• Timestamp
• Final “arrived safely” message when journey ends.
🔹 Does the receiver need the app? ▼
No! The SMS is a plain text message with a Google Maps link. The receiver only needs a phone that can receive SMS and open a web link. No app installation required.
🔹 What are the SMS interval options? ▼
You can choose from:
• Every 10 minutes
• Every 15 minutes (default)
• Every 30 minutes
• Every 60 minutes
Pick the interval that matches your journey length and comfort level.
🔹 How do I end a journey safely? ▼
When you arrive at your destination, open the app and enter your 4-digit End PIN. This will:
• Stop all automatic SMS updates
• Send a final “arrived safely” SMS to all contacts with your exact location and total journey duration
• Save the journey in your history.
🔹 What is the Distress PIN and how does it work? ▼
The Distress PIN is a secret 4-digit code you set. If you are under threat, enter this PIN instead of the end PIN. The app will:
• End the journey (looks normal)
• Silently send an SOS emergency alert to all contacts
• Include your current location and a distress message
• Show no indication on the screen that an emergency alert was sent.

Yes, WakaSafe works on Android 8.0 (Oreo) and above. The APK size is approximately 30.35 MB. It works on all major Nigerian networks and requires SMS permission to send location updates.

🔹 Is my location shared with anyone else? ▼
No. Your location is only sent to the emergency contacts you have explicitly added. WakaSafe does not share your data with third parties. Your privacy is protected.
🔹 What happens if I enter the wrong PIN? ▼
If you enter an incorrect PIN, the app will not proceed. You can try again. After multiple failed attempts, the app may temporarily lock for security. Always set a PIN you can easily remember.
🔹 Can I change my PIN later? ▼
Yes. You can reset your PIN at any time from the app settings. You will need to confirm your current PIN before setting a new one. This adds an extra layer of security.
🔹 Does WakaSafe work in areas with no network? ▼
WakaSafe requires a cellular network to send SMS. In areas with no network at all, SMS cannot be delivered. However, the app will retry when network becomes available. The app itself works offline.
🔹 What languages does WakaSafe support? ▼
WakaSafe is designed for Nigerians and supports multiple languages: English, Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, Pidgin, and French. You can select your preferred language in the app settings.
🔹 How do I add emergency contacts? ▼
From the dashboard, tap the “Emergency Contacts” section and select “Add Contact.” You can manually enter a phone number or pick from your device contacts. Each contact will receive all journey updates.
🔹 Will I be charged for the SMS sent? ▼
Yes, standard SMS charges from your network provider apply for each message sent. WakaSafe does not charge you for the SMS itself. The app sends SMS using your phone’s native SMS service — you will be billed by your network provider.
🔹 Can I see my journey history? ▼
Yes. All past journeys are saved in the app. You can view details like route, vehicle, duration, number of SMS sent, and timestamps. You also have the option to back up your history to the cloud.
🔹 What is the “Safe at Base” status? ▼
“Safe at Base” means you have successfully ended your journey and are no longer in transit. It indicates that you have arrived safely and no active tracking is in progress.
🔹 How do I activate the silent SOS? ▼
To activate the silent SOS, simply enter your Distress PIN when prompted to end a journey. The app will process it as an end journey, but secretly sends an emergency alert to all your contacts. No visual alert appears on your phone.

🔹 How do I change the SMS interval? ▼
You can change the SMS interval before starting a journey. In the “Plan Journey” screen, choose your preferred interval from the dropdown: 10, 15, 30, or 60 minutes. The interval cannot be changed after the journey has started.
🔹 Can I use WakaSafe for inter-state travel? ▼
Absolutely. WakaSafe is designed for journeys of any length — from within the same city to long inter-state trips. The app works everywhere in Nigeria as long as there is network coverage for SMS.
🔹 What permissions does WakaSafe require? ▼
WakaSafe requires:
• SMS permission (to send location updates)
• Location permission (to get your GPS coordinates)
• Contacts permission (optional, to select emergency contacts from your phone)
• Notification permission (for alerts).
🔹 How is the app different from other safety apps? ▼
WakaSafe works offline via SMS — no internet required. It is built for Nigerian roads where data is often unreliable. It uses a three-PIN system (start, end, distress) and sends detailed location updates with vehicle information, making it uniquely suited for the Nigerian travel experience.
🔹 Can I share my journey with multiple people? ▼
Yes. You can add as many emergency contacts as you like. Every SMS update is sent to all of them simultaneously. This way, your entire family or close friends can monitor your journey together.
